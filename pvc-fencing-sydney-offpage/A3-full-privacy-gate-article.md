A full privacy PVC gate provides the maximum level of visual screening available in a gate product. The panel construction uses solid boards with no visible gaps, preventing any view through the gate face. This makes it the preferred choice for properties where privacy at the driveway or entry point is a priority.

This article provides a practical overview of full privacy PVC gates: what they include, how they perform, and what to consider before installation.

## Panel Construction

A full privacy gate panel uses boards pressed together to form a solid face. The boards are housed within a frame that provides structural rigidity. In a PVC gate, the frame and boards are manufactured from the same material, and the gate is usually supplied as a completed panel ready for hanging on hinges.

The solid panel construction means the gate is heavier per unit area than a semi privacy or railing gate of the same size. This affects the hinge and hardware specifications, which must be rated to the panel weight and dimensions.

## Where Full Privacy Gates Are Used

Full privacy gates are most commonly used at driveway entries on residential properties where the homeowner does not want pedestrians or passing vehicles to see into the property. They are also used on side gates that lead to rear yards, where a degree of separation from the street is valued.

In some applications, a full privacy gate is used at a business entry or commercial property where the contents of the yard are not intended to be visible from the footpath or road.

## Single Gate vs Sliding Gate

A full privacy single gate uses a swing mechanism. The panel rotates on hinges and swings either inward or outward depending on the installation. For residential driveways, an inward swing is the more common arrangement.

A full privacy sliding gate travels horizontally along the fence line on a track or roller system. It requires a clear run of fence on the stacking side and a level or engineered track surface. For wider driveways or sites where swing clearance is limited, a sliding gate is often the more practical option.

## Hardware and Weight Considerations

The solid panel construction of a full privacy gate means the panel is heavier than an equivalent-sized semi privacy or railing gate. The hinges, hinge post, and latch must all be rated for this additional weight.

Undersized hinges on a heavy panel cause the gate to sag over time. A sagging gate does not close cleanly at the latch and may drag on the ground. Selecting the correct hardware from the outset prevents this outcome.

## Automation

Full privacy single gates and sliding gates are compatible with automation. A swing gate typically uses an arm actuator; a sliding gate uses a chain or rack-and-pinion motor. If automation is planned, confirm the gate and post dimensions with both the gate supplier and the motor supplier before installation to ensure compatibility.

Automated installations may require gate posts with additional reinforcement. Confirming this at the ordering stage avoids the need to reinstall posts after the gate is in place.

## Maintenance

PVC full privacy gates require no painting and are not affected by rust. Cleaning with water is usually sufficient to maintain the surface appearance. The hinge and latch mechanisms benefit from annual lubrication. Inspect all fixings periodically, as thermal expansion and contraction through seasonal temperature changes can cause fixings to loosen over time.
