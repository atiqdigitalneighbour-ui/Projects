A railing double gate creates a wide opening that suits driveways, garden entrances, and access points on properties where a solid gate panel would appear too heavy. The open rail design allows light and air to pass through while still defining the boundary of the property clearly.

Before purchasing a PVC railing double gate, there are several practical points to consider. These relate to gate dimensions, hardware selection, site conditions, and matching the gate to the surrounding fence.

## Gate Width and Opening Clearance

A double gate creates an opening wider than a single gate. The total width is determined by the combined width of both gate panels plus the gap between them when closed. Before specifying a gate width, measure the clear opening between the two hinge posts.

Account for the hardware on each side. Hinges add width at each hinge post, and the latch mechanism occupies width at the centre. The actual clear passage through the gate will be slightly narrower than the distance between the posts.

If the opening needs to accommodate a vehicle, measure the widest vehicle that will pass through regularly. This is often a caravan, trailer, or large utility vehicle rather than the family car.

## Hardware Selection for a Double Gate

A double gate requires a latch that connects the two panels at the centre. The latch should be operable from both sides so the gate can be used as a pedestrian entrance as well as a vehicle gate.

The fixed leaf (the panel that does not operate as the main entry) requires a foot bolt or drop rod to hold it in place independently. This drop rod should engage with a receiver in the ground or in a strike plate at the base of the gate opening.

Choose hinges rated for the combined weight of the panel. For a PVC railing gate, the panels are lighter than solid PVC or timber panels, but the leverage effect of a wide gate still requires adequate hinge strength.

## Ground Conditions and Post Setting

The hinge posts for a double gate carry the full load of both panels in use. These posts must be set deep enough and in a footing strong enough to resist the leverage created by the panel width.

On loose or sandy soil, posts may need deeper footings or a concrete collar around the base. On hard substrates, a surface-mount anchor bracket may be an alternative, but this must be confirmed against the manufacturer's load ratings.

On sloped ground, the gate opening may not be level. A railing double gate can be raked to follow a gentle slope, but a steep gradient may require the gate to be set at a level height with the gap between the gate bottom and the ground managed separately.

## Matching the Gate to the Fence Profile

A railing gate should share the same rail dimensions, post style, and overall height as the adjacent railing fence. When the profiles do not match, the gate is visually inconsistent and the transition from fence to gate is obvious.

If ordering a gate to match an existing fence, confirm the product specifications for both at the time of purchase. Suppliers who carry the same product range should be able to provide a confirmed match.

## Maintenance Expectations

PVC railing gates require minimal maintenance. The material does not require painting, does not rust, and does not absorb moisture. Cleaning with water removes surface dust and grime.

The hardware components, particularly hinges and the drop rod mechanism, benefit from annual lubrication. Inspect fixings periodically and tighten any that have loosened through use or thermal expansion.

## Summary

Selecting a PVC railing double gate involves measuring the opening accurately, choosing hardware rated for the gate size, confirming the post footing requirements, and matching the gate profile to the surrounding fence.
