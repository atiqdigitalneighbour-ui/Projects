A picket style double gate creates a wide entrance with the open, traditional appearance associated with picket fencing. The design uses vertical pickets with defined spaces between them, providing a clear boundary without enclosing the property fully. This style is a common choice for front gardens, driveways on residential properties, and access points within landscaped areas.

This article covers the key considerations for selecting a picket style PVC double gate, including sizing, hardware, profile matching, and site preparation.

## Understanding the Picket Design

A picket gate uses vertical boards or pickets spaced at regular intervals within a gate frame. The pickets are fixed and do not move independently. The space between the pickets is part of the design and contributes to the open, traditional aesthetic.

The overall height and the picket spacing determine how the gate reads visually. Taller gates with closely spaced pickets appear more formal. Shorter gates with wider spacing appear lighter and more relaxed. Selecting a profile that suits the surrounding fence and the property style is part of the buying decision.

## Why a Double Gate Rather Than a Single Gate?

A double gate creates a wider opening than a single gate. This is useful for driveways, equipment access, or where the entry needs to accommodate wide loads such as caravans or trailers.

Double gates also create a more symmetrical visual presentation at the entry, which suits a front boundary where the entrance is a visible feature of the property.

## Measuring the Opening

Measure the clear distance between the two hinge posts before specifying the gate. A double gate occupies this space, with each panel taking approximately half the total width. Account for the hardware at the hinge points and at the centre latch, which reduces the clear passage width slightly.

For a driveway gate, confirm the opening is wide enough for the widest vehicle that will use it. Measure the vehicle at its widest point, including mirrors and any attachments.

## Hardware for a Picket Double Gate

A double gate requires a centre latch that holds both panels together when closed. The latch should be operable from both sides if the gate is used for pedestrian access as well as vehicle access.

The fixed panel requires a foot bolt or drop rod to hold it independently in position. Choose stainless or galvanised hardware for outdoor use to prevent corrosion over years of exposure.

Hinges must be rated for the panel weight and dimensions. Even though PVC is lighter than timber, the hinge must carry the full panel weight and the leverage effect of the panel width throughout the gate's working life.

## Matching the Gate to the Fence

A picket double gate should match the surrounding picket fence in board width, spacing, height, and post style. When gates and fence panels do not match, the entry looks inconsistent against the rest of the boundary.

## Post Requirements

Gate posts for a double gate carry more load than standard fence posts. They should be set deeper and in a stronger footing. On soft ground, a concrete collar around the post base is recommended.

Confirm the post dimensions recommended by the gate supplier for the gate size you are ordering. Using undersized posts for a double gate is one of the most common causes of gate sag and long-term misalignment.

## Maintenance

PVC picket gates require no painting and are resistant to moisture and UV degradation when manufactured with UV stabilisation. Cleaning with water maintains the surface appearance. Hardware components should be lubricated annually and inspected for loosening fixings.
