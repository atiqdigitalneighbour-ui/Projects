A double gate can define a property's entrance more visibly than any other single fencing element. When combined with a semi privacy fence, the gate creates a functional and visually considered boundary that provides partial screening without completely closing off the property from the street.

Selecting the correct double gate, however, involves more than choosing a colour or a panel style. The gate width, the opening mechanism, the matching fence profile, and the hardware all affect how the gate performs over its working life. This guide works through each consideration in a structured way.

## Understanding What Semi Privacy Means

Semi privacy panels use boards or pickets with defined gaps between them. The result is a fence that allows some visibility through the face while still providing a degree of screening. Airflow is maintained, and the fence feels less imposing than a full privacy panel.

A semi privacy double gate uses the same spacing and board design as the adjacent fence. This creates a consistent visual line across the boundary. When the gate panels and fence panels do not match in board width, spacing, or height, the gate becomes visually inconsistent with the surrounding fence.

## Why a Double Gate and Not Two Single Gates?

A double gate and two independently hung single gates create a similar physical opening. The difference lies in how the load is distributed and how the panels operate.

A double gate typically uses one panel as the fixed or primary leaf and one as the operating leaf. The fixed leaf is held in place by a drop rod bolted to the ground or a foot bolt. The operating leaf carries the latch and operates freely.

Two single gates, by contrast, both operate independently. This gives more flexibility for daily use but increases the number of operating components and the potential for misalignment over time.

## Determining the Correct Gate Width

Measure the opening carefully before specifying a gate. A double gate width is the total clear opening between the two hinge posts. The gate itself must be slightly narrower than this to allow for hinges on each side and the gap between the panels when closed.

Common errors include measuring the distance between fence panels rather than between the posts, or not accounting for the gate hardware that adds width to each hinge and latch connection. Incorrect width measurements lead to gates that either bind against the posts or have excessive gaps.

For properties where vehicles need to pass through, check the width of the widest vehicle that will use the entry. Caravans, trailers, and removalist trucks require wider openings than standard cars.

## Post Selection and Setting

A double gate places significant load on the hinge posts. The combined weight of two large PVC panels, plus the leverage created by the panel width, means the hinge post must be set deeper and more securely than a standard fence post.

Consult the gate supplier for the minimum post dimensions and footing depth for the gate panel size you are ordering. In soft or sandy soils, deeper footings with concrete collars are usually required.

## Choosing the Hinge Type

Hinges for a double gate must be rated for the panel weight. Weld-on, bolt-through, and strap hinge styles are all available. For PVC gates, the hinge must attach securely to the gate frame without compressing or cracking the profile.

Over time, hinges that are undersized or incorrectly attached allow the gate to sag. A sagging gate does not close cleanly at the latch and may drag on the ground. Selecting correctly rated hinges from the outset prevents this.

## The Latch and Drop Rod

A double gate needs a latch that bridges the two panels and holds them together when closed. The latch should be operable from both sides if the gate is used as a pedestrian entry as well as a vehicle gate.

The fixed leaf requires a drop rod or foot bolt to hold it in position independently of the operating leaf. The drop rod engages with a receiver in the ground or in the gate frame. Choose a stainless or galvanised drop rod that will not corrode over years of outdoor use.

## Considering Automation

Double gates can be automated using arm-style actuators. Each gate leaf typically requires its own actuator, and the two must be controlled in a paired sequence to open and close correctly.

Automation suits properties where the gate is opened and closed frequently, where weather makes manual operation inconvenient, or where security is a priority. Before automating, confirm that the gate and posts are suitable for the additional load and vibration of a motorised system.

## Matching the Gate to the Existing Fence

If you are adding a double gate to an existing PVC fence, bring the fence profile details to the supplier. A supplier who offers the same fence range should be able to match the board width, spacing, and panel height exactly.

If the fence was installed by a different supplier or the product is no longer available, a close match may be the best achievable result. In this case, review samples against the existing fence in natural light before ordering.

## Maintenance Requirements

PVC gates require minimal maintenance. An occasional clean with water and a soft cloth removes surface dust and grime. Lubricate the hinges and latch mechanism annually to keep the gate operating smoothly.

Inspect the hinge bolts and latch periodically. Over years of use and temperature fluctuation, fixings can loosen. Tightening them before they fail prevents more significant repair work later.

## Summary

Selecting a semi privacy double gate requires attention to width, post specification, hinge rating, latch type, and profile matching. Taking time to measure accurately, specify correctly, and choose appropriate hardware leads to a gate that functions reliably and looks consistent against the surrounding fence.
