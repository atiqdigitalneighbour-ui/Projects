When planning a fence for a home or outdoor space, the gate choice matters as much as the fence itself. A double gate creates a wider opening that suits driveways, side entrances, and areas where larger equipment or vehicles need to pass through. For properties where some visual screening is needed but total enclosure is not the goal, a semi privacy double gate offers a practical middle ground.

This guide looks at what to consider when selecting a double gate for a semi-private setting, covering structure, materials, spacing, and suitability for Sydney conditions.

## What Does Semi Privacy Mean in Fencing?

Semi privacy fencing allows some airflow and partial visibility through the slats or boards while still providing a degree of screening. It is commonly chosen for side fences, garden boundaries, and areas where both neighbours and the street are close.

A double gate in the same style extends this visual consistency across an opening. Because the panels on both sides of the gate match the fence, the overall look remains cohesive. This matters particularly in residential settings where presentation is a priority.

## Why the Gate Opening Size Matters

The width of a double gate affects both function and aesthetics. A standard double gate is generally wider than a single gate, making it suitable for driveways, caravan access, and large garden areas.

Before selecting a gate width, measure the opening carefully. Account for the gate posts and any hardware that may reduce the clear passage width. If the opening is irregular, a custom gate size may be required rather than an off-the-shelf panel.

## PVC as a Gate Material

PVC is used widely in gate and fence construction because it does not require painting, does not rust, and holds its colour over time. In coastal and humid conditions common in Sydney, PVC retains its structural integrity better than untreated timber.

A semi privacy PVC double gate uses a design where the boards or pickets are spaced to allow some visibility through the face of the gate. This is different from a full privacy panel, which has no visible gaps. The exact spacing on a semi privacy gate determines the balance between openness and screening.

## Gate Hardware and Hinge Requirements

A double gate requires a suitable latch and hinge set to carry the combined weight of both panels. The hardware must be rated for the panel weight and width. Under-specified hardware leads to sagging, misalignment, and difficulty closing the gate over time.

Stainless steel or galvanised hardware is recommended for outdoor use in New South Wales. This reduces the risk of corrosion and ensures the gate continues to open and close correctly through seasonal changes.

## Matching the Gate to the Fence

When ordering a semi privacy double gate to sit alongside an existing fence, confirm the board spacing, panel height, and profile match the fence sections. Even small differences in slat width or spacing can make the gate appear mismatched against the surrounding fence.

## Considering Ground Conditions

On sloped ground, a standard double gate may not close evenly due to the gradient. A raked gate or a two-panel configuration with independent heights may be needed. If the ground is uneven, discuss this with the supplier before placing an order.

Where the ground is soft or prone to movement, the gate posts need to be set deep enough to prevent lean. Posts that shift over time cause the gate to drag or fail to latch properly.

## Installation and Spacing

Before installation, plan the hinge side and opening direction. A double gate usually opens inward to avoid blocking the footpath or road. Confirm local council requirements before installing a gate that swings outward over a public area.

The gap between the two gate panels when closed should be minimal to maintain the screening function of the gate. A centre drop rod on one panel holds it in position while the other panel operates independently.

## Finding a Reliable Supplier

When selecting a gate supplier, look for one that can confirm product dimensions in advance, provide hardware specifications, and offer guidance on installation requirements. The supplier should also be able to match the gate to your existing fence profile if applicable.
