Not all fencing projects are permanent, and not all require full screening. A railing fence provides definition and containment without blocking the view, while a temporary fence serves short-term needs at events or construction sites. Both have specific installation requirements, and both benefit from careful planning before work begins.

This checklist covers the key decisions and measurements to make before ordering either a PVC railing fence or a temporary PVC fence for a Sydney property or site.

## Step 1: Define the Purpose of the Fence

Before selecting a fence type, identify what the fence needs to do. A railing fence is chosen for delineation, aesthetics, or safety near elevated areas. A temporary fence is chosen for short-term site security, crowd management, or event containment.

If the fence is permanent, a railing product is more appropriate. If the project lasts weeks or months rather than years, a temporary system saves cost and simplifies removal.

## Step 2: Measure the Site

For a railing fence, measure the total linear metres of the boundary. Note any corners, gates, or changes in ground level. Railing fence panels come in standard widths, so the total run will determine how many panels and posts are required.

For a temporary fence, note the perimeter of the area to be enclosed and identify any access points where gate panels will be needed. Temporary fencing is modular and joins at panel clips, so the perimeter measurement drives the panel count directly.

## Step 3: Check Ground and Surface Conditions

A railing fence for permanent installation requires posts set in concrete footings. Check for underground services before digging. On hard surfaces such as concrete or pavers, surface-mount post brackets may be an alternative to core drilling.

Temporary fencing typically uses weighted feet rather than ground anchors. Confirm that the surface can support the foot weight and that no trip hazard exists for pedestrians. On soft ground, the feet may sink and require additional support.

## Step 4: Identify Gate Requirements

Railing fences often include a matching single or double gate for access. The gate should match the panel height and profile for visual consistency. Confirm gate opening direction and latch type before ordering.

Temporary fencing uses pedestrian or vehicle gate panels that slot into the standard panel system. If a vehicle gate is needed, confirm the width required for the largest vehicle expected to pass through.

## Step 5: Consider Visibility and Screening

Railing fencing is designed to be open and visible. It does not provide screening. If the project requires some visual separation, a semi-privacy or privacy panel may be more appropriate.

Temporary fencing can be fitted with shade cloth or screening mesh if visual blocking is needed on-site. This is common on construction sites where dust or sight-line management is required.

## Step 6: Review Council and Site Requirements

In New South Wales, certain fencing installations near roads, boundaries, or heritage areas require council approval. For commercial sites, the principal contractor is usually responsible for ensuring temporary fencing meets relevant safety standards.

For events on public land, site fencing requirements are generally included in the event permit. Confirm the required specifications before ordering.

## Step 7: Plan for Removal and Storage

For a temporary fence, decide in advance how and where panels will be stored or returned after use. Hire systems include delivery, installation, and collection. Purchase systems require transport and storage space.

For a permanent railing fence, no removal plan is needed, but consider future access for maintenance of the ground or landscaping within the fenced area.

## Summary Checklist

- Define the purpose: permanent delineation or short-term site use
- Measure the total perimeter in linear metres
- Check ground conditions and underground services
- Identify gate positions, widths, and types
- Confirm screening requirements
- Check council or event permit requirements
- Plan for removal or storage if temporary

Working through each of these points before placing an order reduces the risk of receiving the wrong quantity, the wrong product type, or a product that does not suit the site conditions.
