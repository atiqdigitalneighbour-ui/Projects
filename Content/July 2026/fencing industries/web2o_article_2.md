# Planning an Automatic Gate Installation for Commercial and Industrial Properties

Automatic gates offer security, convenience, and a professional appearance for commercial and industrial properties. However, they also involve significant planning, structural requirements, electrical work, and ongoing maintenance responsibilities. Installing an automatic gate system without proper preparation can lead to operational failures, safety risks, and unexpected costs.

This article outlines what property owners and facility managers need to consider before proceeding with a gate installation on a commercial or industrial site.

---

## Why Gate Installations Require Careful Planning

Unlike a standard pedestrian gate, an automatic gate is a mechanical and electrical system. It operates through motors, control panels, sensors, and power connections. Each component affects the reliability and safety of the overall system.

The approach to a commercial gate installation is therefore much closer to a building services project than a standard fencing job. It requires coordination between fencing contractors, electricians, and in some cases building or planning authorities.

## Types of Gates for Commercial and Industrial Sites

### Sliding Gates

Sliding gates operate by moving horizontally along a track or cantilever system. They are well suited to sites where there is limited clearance in front of the gate opening. The gate requires a clear run of space to the side equal to at least the width of the gate leaf.

Sliding gates are commonly used in industrial estates, warehouses, transport depots, and larger commercial properties. For more detail on [automatic gates Canterbury](https://www.fencingindustries.co.nz/products-services/gates) options, including sliding and custom fabricated designs, it is worth reviewing what experienced contractors can offer.

### Swing Gates

Swing gates operate on hinges and open inward or outward. They require clear space in the direction of travel. Swing gates are suitable for lower-cycle applications and smaller openings.

They tend to have a more traditional aesthetic but are not ideal in situations where vehicles stop close to the gate, where wind loading is high, or where space is restricted.

## Key Considerations Before Installation

### Site Access and Traffic Volume

The volume and type of vehicles accessing the site each day will determine the appropriate gate type and motor specification. A gate receiving a small number of vehicle movements per day has very different requirements from one handling heavy transport around the clock.

Specifying a light-duty motor on a high-cycle application leads to premature failure and ongoing maintenance expense.

### Power Supply

Automatic gates require a reliable power supply. On some sites, extending the power supply to the gate location requires significant electrical work. Battery backup systems and solar-assisted options are available but add cost and maintenance considerations.

Confirm the electrical requirements with your contractor and electrician before selecting a gate system.

### Safety Devices

Automatic gate installations must include appropriate safety devices. These typically include:

- obstacle detection sensors to prevent the gate from closing on a vehicle or person
- safety edges on the leading face of the gate leaf
- warning lights or audible signals in some environments
- manual release mechanisms for power failure or emergency access

### Integration with Broader Site Security

Gates on [industrial fencing](https://www.fencingindustries.co.nz/sectors/industrial) projects form part of a wider perimeter security strategy. Access control integration, including intercoms, keypads, key fobs, swipe cards, or remote controls, should be specified at the design stage. Retrofitting access control is almost always more expensive than including it from the outset.

---

## Custom Fabrication

Many commercial and industrial gate projects require custom-fabricated gate leaves to match specific widths, heights, or aesthetic requirements. Standard off-the-shelf gate kits are rarely suitable for larger or more demanding sites.

Custom fabrication allows the gate to be built to the exact dimensions and specification required, ensuring a correct fit and proper structural performance.

---

## Maintenance Responsibilities

An automatic gate system requires regular maintenance to remain safe and reliable. Typical maintenance tasks include:

- checking and lubricating drive mechanisms and bearings
- testing safety sensors and auto-reverse functions
- inspecting the gate structure for signs of wear, corrosion, or damage
- checking electrical connections and control panels
- reviewing and updating access control credentials

Agreeing a maintenance schedule with your [fencing installers](https://www.fencingindustries.co.nz) at the outset is good practice. Some contractors offer service agreements covering routine maintenance and priority call-out.

---

## Summary

Automatic gate installations are complex projects that require careful specification, professional installation, and ongoing maintenance. Getting the planning right from the beginning reduces the likelihood of operational problems and unexpected costs.

Whether you are installing a sliding gate on an industrial site or a custom fabricated gate at a commercial entrance, working with an experienced contractor who understands the full scope of the project will produce the best result.
