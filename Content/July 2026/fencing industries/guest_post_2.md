# Automatic and Sliding Gates: A Decision Framework for Property Managers

The gate at the entrance to a commercial or industrial property does more than open and close. It controls who enters and exits, communicates the professionalism of the business, and forms a critical element of the site's physical security strategy. Getting the specification and installation right from the start avoids operational disruptions and unnecessary costs over the life of the asset.

This article is intended for property managers, facility operators, and project managers who are evaluating gate options for a new development or an upgrade of an existing system.

---

## The Role of a Gate in a Commercial Security Strategy

Physical security for a commercial or industrial site typically involves several layers. The perimeter fence defines the boundary and provides the first passive barrier. The gate is the controlled point at which that barrier can be opened and closed.

An automatic gate brings two significant advantages over a manually operated gate. First, it removes the need for a staff member to open and close the gate for every vehicle movement, which can be a significant operational burden on busy sites. Second, it creates a mechanism through which access can be logged, controlled, and restricted through integration with an access control system.

These advantages come with corresponding responsibilities. Automatic gate systems must be maintained, inspected, and managed correctly to remain safe and reliable.

---

## Choosing Between Sliding and Swing Gates

The two most common automatic gate configurations in commercial and industrial settings are sliding gates and swing gates.

### Sliding Gates

A sliding gate moves horizontally on a track or cantilever system. Cantilever sliding gates, which do not require a bottom track, are widely used in commercial settings because they are cleaner to install, easier to maintain, and less vulnerable to debris and ice affecting operation.

Sliding gates are the preferred choice when:

- the available clearance in front of the gate opening is limited
- the gate opening is wide
- the cycle rate (number of openings per day) is high
- vehicles stop close to the gate on the road side

Industrial estates, logistics facilities, and manufacturing sites commonly use sliding gates for vehicle access.

### Swing Gates

Swing gates operate on hinges and require clear space in the direction of swing. They are suitable for lower-traffic applications and smaller openings. Paired swing gates can create a wider opening without the lateral run required by a sliding gate.

Swing gates are more vulnerable to wind loading than sliding gates. In exposed locations, the motor and hinge specifications must account for this.

## Custom Fabricated Gate Solutions

## Motor Specification

Selecting the correct motor for the gate is a critical part of the specification process. The motor must be rated for:

- the weight of the gate leaf
- the expected cycle rate per day
- the duty cycle (how frequently the motor operates within a given period)
- the environmental conditions on site

An undersized motor on a heavy gate with high cycle rates will fail prematurely. Conversely, an oversized motor on a light gate may present safety risks if the stopping force and auto-reverse sensitivity are not correctly calibrated.

## Safety Requirements

Automatic gate installations must incorporate safety devices to prevent injury. Key safety requirements include:

**Obstacle detection.** Sensors on the leading and trailing edges of the gate detect obstruction and stop or reverse the gate movement. Photo-electric cells are commonly used in addition to contact-based safety edges.

**Manual release.** A mechanical manual release allows the gate to be opened in the event of a power failure or motor fault. The release must be accessible to authorised personnel.

**Audible or visual warning.** In some environments, a warning alarm or flashing light that activates as the gate begins to move is required to alert pedestrians.

**Safe pedestrian access.** If pedestrian access and vehicle access share the same gate opening, a separate pedestrian gate with appropriate segregation should be incorporated into the design.

## Access Control Integration

Modern automatic gate systems can be integrated with a wide range of access control technologies:

- wireless key fobs
- keypads with PIN entry
- proximity card readers
- intercom systems with visual monitoring
- vehicle loop detectors for automatic exit
- number plate recognition systems

The level of sophistication appropriate for a site depends on the security requirements, the volume of authorised users, and the budget available.

---

## Maintenance Planning

Automatic gate systems should be serviced at least annually by a qualified technician. The service visit should include:

- mechanical inspection of gate structure, hinges, and track
- motor and gearbox inspection and lubrication
- electrical connection and control panel inspection
- testing of all safety devices including auto-reverse function
- review of access control credentials and removal of outdated users
