Semi privacy fencing sits between open picket fencing and full privacy fencing in terms of visual screening. The panels allow some visibility through the face of the fence while still providing a degree of separation and wind protection. For many residential properties, this balance of openness and screening makes semi privacy fencing the most practical choice.

This article explains what semi privacy PVC fencing is, where it works best, and what to consider when selecting it.

## How Semi Privacy Fencing Differs from Other Panel Types

Picket fencing uses widely spaced vertical boards or pickets. It provides little visual screening but clearly marks a boundary. Full privacy fencing uses boards placed tightly together with no visible gaps. It provides complete visual screening but blocks airflow and light.

Semi privacy fencing uses boards with a defined gap between them. The gap is narrower than a picket fence but wider than a full privacy panel. This allows some light and air to pass through while obscuring the view through the fence from a normal standing position.

## Where Semi Privacy Fencing Is Commonly Used

Semi privacy fencing is popular for side and rear boundaries in residential settings. It provides screening from neighbours without creating the enclosed feeling of a full privacy fence.

It is also used as a front fence on properties where the owner wants a more considered boundary than a picket fence but does not want to fully enclose the front garden. In this application, the fence adds street presence without shutting out the streetscape entirely.

In commercial or light industrial settings, semi privacy fencing can define boundaries around outdoor storage or service areas while allowing some visibility for surveillance or ventilation.

## The Role of Board Width and Spacing

The appearance and screening level of a semi privacy panel depend on the width of the boards and the size of the gap between them. Wider boards with smaller gaps provide more screening. Narrower boards with larger gaps provide more openness.

When comparing products, ask the supplier for the exact board width and gap dimension. This information allows you to compare different products on a consistent basis rather than relying on descriptions such as "semi privacy" which can vary between manufacturers.

## PVC as a Fencing Material

PVC fencing does not require painting or sealing. The material is not affected by moisture in the way that timber is, and it does not rust or corrode. In climates with high UV exposure and significant rainfall, PVC retains its appearance over many years without the maintenance that painted timber requires.

UV-stabilised PVC is preferred for external applications. Ask the supplier to confirm the UV stability of the product before purchasing.

## Post and Panel Installation Basics

A semi privacy fence installs on posts set at regular intervals. The panel width determines the post spacing. Most panels are manufactured in standard lengths, so the post spacing should be set to match the panel width exactly.

On level ground, installation is straightforward. On sloped ground, the panels can be stepped or raked depending on the gradient and the panel design. Stepped installation keeps each panel horizontal and creates a stair-step appearance. Raked installation allows the panel to follow the slope continuously.

## Gate Compatibility

Semi privacy fence products usually include matching gate panels in single, double, and sliding configurations. Ordering gates from the same product range ensures that the board width, spacing, and height match the fence panels exactly.

When selecting a gate, confirm the gate hardware is specified for the gate panel weight and dimensions. Under-specified hardware is one of the most common causes of gate failure over time.
