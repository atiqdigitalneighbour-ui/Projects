## Practical Advice for Long-Haul Flights

Surviving a long-haul flight requires a strategic approach to comfort and hydration. The cabin environment is extremely dry, which can leave you feeling fatigued upon arrival. You should drink plenty of water and avoid excessive caffeine during the journey.

Movement is also essential during extended periods of sitting. You should take regular walks around the cabin when it is safe to do so. Simple stretching exercises in your seat can help maintain circulation and reduce the risk of stiffness.

Adjusting to the new time zone should begin as soon as you board the aircraft. You can set your watch to the destination time and try to sleep according to that schedule. This proactive approach significantly reduces the impact of jet lag.
