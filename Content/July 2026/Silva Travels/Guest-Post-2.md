# What to Know Before Flying to Thiruvananthapuram from New Zealand

Travelling to southern India presents a profoundly different cultural experience for New Zealanders. The region of Kerala is renowned for its lush landscapes and historical significance. Thiruvananthapuram, the capital city, serves as the primary gateway to this vibrant state. It offers a captivating introduction to the diverse traditions of the Indian subcontinent.

The journey requires significant preparation due to the vast geographical distance. You must navigate multiple time zones and complex transit routes. Arriving well-prepared ensures that you can immediately begin enjoying the rich local culture.

## Understanding the Journey

The flight path to southern India is typically long and involves at least one major layover. You must prepare for an extended period of travel. Selecting a comfortable airline and an efficient transit hub is crucial for minimising fatigue.

When you arrange [Flights from Auckland to Thiruvananthapuram](https://silvatravel.co.nz/destinations/flights-from-auckland-to-thiruvananthapuram), you are booking a journey that crosses several continents. The most common routes connect through major Asian or Middle Eastern airports. You should review the transit times carefully to avoid excessively long waits between flights.

Jet lag is an inevitable consequence of this journey. You can mitigate its effects by adjusting your sleep schedule a few days before departure. Staying hydrated during the flight also helps your body adapt to the changing time zones more rapidly.

## Visa Requirements and Documentation

Entry into India requires specific documentation that you must arrange well in advance. Most New Zealand citizens are eligible for an electronic visa for tourism purposes. The application process is entirely online, but it requires careful attention to detail.

You must ensure your passport has at least six months of validity remaining from your date of arrival. You will also need a digital photograph that meets strict specifications. Any errors in your application can result in significant delays or denial of entry.

It is advisable to print physical copies of your visa approval and your return flight itinerary. Immigration officials may request to see these documents upon arrival. Having them readily available demonstrates that you are a prepared and compliant traveller.

## Health and Safety Considerations

Consulting a medical professional before travelling to India is essential. They can advise you on recommended vaccinations and health precautions based on your specific itinerary. You should schedule this consultation several weeks before your departure date.

Food and water hygiene require careful attention during your stay. You should only consume bottled or properly purified water. Eating freshly cooked, hot food is the best way to avoid gastrointestinal issues while exploring local cuisine.

Comprehensive travel insurance is mandatory for this type of journey. You must ensure your policy covers medical evacuation, as local healthcare facilities may vary in quality. Understanding your insurance coverage provides peace of mind throughout your holiday.

## Cultural Etiquette in Kerala

Kerala is a culturally conservative region, and visitors should dress modestly. Both men and women should cover their shoulders and knees when in public spaces. This is particularly important when visiting religious sites or rural communities.

Public displays of affection are generally frowned upon. You should also be mindful of your interactions with local residents, ensuring you communicate with respect and politeness. Learning a few basic phrases in Malayalam, the local language, is always appreciated.

Removing your footwear before entering a home or a place of worship is a strict custom. You should carry slip-on shoes to make this process easier. Observing these local traditions demonstrates respect and often leads to warmer interactions with the local people.

## Managing Finances and Currency

The local currency is the Indian Rupee, and it is a closed currency. This means you cannot legally obtain it outside of India. You must exchange your money or withdraw cash from an ATM upon arrival at the airport.

While major hotels and restaurants accept credit cards, cash is essential for smaller transactions. You will need small denominations for transport, local markets, and tipping. It is wise to inform your bank of your travel plans to prevent your cards from being blocked.

Bargaining is common in local markets, but it should be conducted politely. It is part of the cultural experience rather than an aggressive negotiation. Understanding the fair value of items helps you navigate these interactions smoothly.
