## Ignoring Passport Validity Requirements

One of the most devastating mistakes is failing to check your passport validity before booking. Most countries require at least six months of validity remaining from your date of entry. If your passport expires sooner, you may be denied boarding regardless of your ticket status.

This requirement catches many travellers by surprise, particularly those making spontaneous bookings. You must check the specific entry requirements of your destination country immediately upon deciding to travel. Renewing a passport can take several weeks, so early action is essential.

You should also ensure that your passport has sufficient blank pages for entry stamps. Some countries require a minimum number of empty pages for immigration processing. Arriving at the airport without meeting these requirements results in an immediate and costly denial of travel.

## Overlooking Transit Visa Requirements

Many international routes involve a change of aircraft at a foreign airport. Travellers frequently assume that transit passengers do not require any documentation for the connecting country. This assumption is incorrect for several major transit hubs around the world.

You must research whether your transit country requires a specific visa for airport layovers. Some nations mandate a transit visa even if you do not intend to leave the airport terminal. Failing to obtain this document can strand you at your departure gate.

When you book [Flights from Auckland to Dubai](https://silvatravel.co.nz/destinations/flights-from-auckland-to-dubai), for example, you should verify the transit policies of any intermediate stops. Understanding these requirements in advance prevents stressful encounters with immigration officials during your journey.

## Booking Non-Refundable Fares Without Insurance

The allure of a discounted non-refundable fare is powerful. However, purchasing such a ticket without travel insurance is a significant financial risk. Unexpected illness, family emergencies, or workplace obligations can force you to cancel at any moment.

Travel insurance that includes trip cancellation coverage provides essential protection. You must purchase this coverage before or immediately after booking your flights. Delaying the purchase can void certain policy benefits.

You should read the cancellation terms of your insurance policy carefully. Not all reasons for cancellation are covered equally. Understanding the specific conditions ensures that your expectations align with the actual protection you have purchased.

## Failing to Compare Total Journey Costs

A common error is focusing exclusively on the ticket price while ignoring the total cost of the journey. Budget airlines frequently charge separately for checked baggage, seat selection, and onboard meals. These additional fees can transform an apparently cheap fare into an expensive booking.

You must calculate the full cost of your journey, including all supplementary charges, before confirming your purchase. Comparing the total expense across different airlines often reveals that a slightly higher base fare offers significantly better overall value.

The quality of the transit experience also affects your total journey cost. An airline offering a free meal and generous baggage allowance may represent better value than a budget carrier charging for every additional service. You must evaluate the complete travel experience rather than the headline fare alone.

## Neglecting to Verify Booking Details

After completing an online booking, you must immediately verify every detail of your confirmation. Check the passenger names, travel dates, flight numbers, and routing carefully. Even a single character error in your name can cause problems at check-in.

Date errors are particularly common when booking across different time zones. A flight departing at midnight on the 15th might actually require you to be at the airport on the evening of the 14th. You must ensure absolute clarity about your departure and arrival times.

If you discover an error, you must contact the airline or your booking agent immediately. Most carriers offer a brief correction window after purchase. Delaying this contact can result in expensive amendment fees or the need to purchase an entirely new ticket.
