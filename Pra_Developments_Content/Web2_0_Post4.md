## Identifying the Right Location

The very first step on your checklist is identifying the right location and understanding the local tenant demand. You need to know what type of housing is most sought after by renters in that specific area. An area with good schools, growing shopping centres, and reliable public transport links is generally considered a safe bet for consistent rental demand.

## Factoring in Ongoing Costs

A thorough investment checklist must also include a realistic assessment of ongoing holding costs. It is not enough to simply calculate the mortgage repayments. You must factor in council rates, water charges, landlord insurance, property management fees, and a buffer for routine maintenance. Failing to account for these ongoing expenses is a common mistake that can turn a seemingly profitable property into a financial burden. Ensure your rental yield calculations reflect these true costs.
