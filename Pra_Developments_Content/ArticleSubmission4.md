## Misunderstanding Contract Inclusions

The most frequent mistake is failing to understand exactly what is included in the contract. The term "fully finished" can mean different things to different builders. Some contracts might include landscaping and window treatments, while others consider these items as optional extras that incur additional fees. 

Always ask for a detailed list of inclusions before signing any documents. ## Choosing Solely Based on the Lowest Quote

Another common error is choosing a builder based solely on the lowest price. While staying within budget is important, an unusually low quote often indicates that the builder intends to use inferior materials or cut corners during construction. 

Quality craftsmanship costs money. It is far better to pay a slightly higher initial price for a well-built home than to face expensive repairs and maintenance issues shortly after moving in. Always compare the value offered, rather than just the final figure.

## Ignoring the Builder's Industry Track Record

Ignoring a builder's track record is a risk you cannot afford to take. You should always investigate their past projects and speak to previous clients if possible. A reputable builder will be proud of their work and happy to provide references.

## Skipping Physical Property Inspections

Many buyers also make the mistake of not visiting display homes or completed projects. Looking at floor plans on paper is not enough to gauge the quality of the finish. 

Walk through a physical home to inspect the standard of the tiling, the quality of the cabinetry, and the overall feel of the space. This physical inspection will tell you much more about a builder's capabilities than a glossy brochure ever could, helping you make a more objective decision.
